# AI Detection Bypass - Technical Analysis & Solution

## The Problem

After multiple attempts, we're still seeing **75-82% AI detection** on generated proposals. This is despite:
- Modified system prompts with human writing instructions
- Post-processing vocabulary substitution
- LLM-based rewriting

## Root Cause Analysis

### Why Post-Processing Fails

AI detectors like **Copyleaks**, **Originality.ai**, **GPTZero**, and **Turnitin** don't just look at vocabulary. They analyze:

1. **Perplexity (Token Probability)**
   - LLMs generate tokens with specific probability patterns
   - Each token choice follows a predictable distribution
   - This creates a mathematical "fingerprint" that CANNOT be removed by post-processing

2. **Burstiness**
   - Human writing varies dramatically in complexity
   - AI maintains uniform complexity throughout
   - Sentence-level entropy patterns differ

3. **Semantic Coherence**
   - AI text is "too perfect" - logical flow is too consistent
   - Humans jump around, make tangential points, circle back

4. **Statistical Fingerprints**
   - Token distribution patterns unique to LLMs
   - Trained classifiers can identify these patterns

### The Mathematical Reality

```
AI Text Generation:
  P(token_n | token_1...token_n-1) follows LLM probability distribution

AI Detection:
  Classifier trained to identify LLM probability patterns

Post-Processing:
  Changes vocabulary but NOT the underlying probability distribution
  The "fingerprint" remains in sentence structure, flow, coherence
```

**Conclusion**: No amount of word substitution can change the fact that the text was originally generated by an LLM.
